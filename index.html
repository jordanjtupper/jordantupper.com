About

Who is Jordan Tupper?

Jordan Tupper is a Baton Rouge, Louisiana musician, bassist, vocalist, music educator, and bandleader known for live performance work across rock, funk, soul, jazz standards, worship music, and private event entertainment.

Jordan performs regularly with several active Baton Rouge music projects, including Night Hog, Cover 6 Band, and Standley, Tupper & Petersen. His musical work spans restaurant residencies, festivals, private events, corporate gatherings, worship settings, and live music venues throughout south Louisiana and the surrounding region.

As a music educator, Jordan was recognized as a 2024 Grammy Music Educator Award National Quarterfinalist. His professional background includes classroom teaching, band direction, program development, team leadership, event production, and community engagement. He also holds an LSU Digital Marketing Certificate and HubSpot certifications in social media and email marketing.

His work brings together performance, teaching, bandleading, and community service across Baton Rouge and the surrounding region.

Background

Music Education & Professional Leadership

Jordan Tupper's professional background is rooted in music education, program management, team leadership, and community development. He has served as a band director, classroom teacher, performer, worship musician, and community leader in both Tennessee and Louisiana.

He was recognized as a national quarterfinalist for the Grammy Music Educator Award, an honor presented by the Recording Academy and GRAMMY Museum to recognize music educators who have made a significant and lasting contribution to music education.

Jordan's experience also includes digital marketing, content strategy, communications, event production, nonprofit service, and business development. Those skills support his current music projects and help connect venues, audiences, and musicians across the Baton Rouge area.
