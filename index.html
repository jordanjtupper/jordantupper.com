About Jordan Tupper

Jordan Tupper is a Baton Rouge, Louisiana–based musician, music educator, and community leader with more than 19 years of professional experience spanning live performance, band direction, program management, and community engagement.

A versatile performer and educator, Jordan Tupper brings a rare combination of artistic depth and organizational leadership to everything he does — whether on stage, in a classroom, or in service of the broader Baton Rouge community. He holds an LSU Digital Marketing Certificate and HubSpot certifications in Social Media Marketing and Email Marketing, and has applied that expertise across music promotion, nonprofit work, and business development.

Jordan Tupper is also a deacon at First Presbyterian Church Baton Rouge and is active in the civic and faith life of the Greater Baton Rouge area.

Professional Background

Jordan Tupper's professional background is rooted in program management, team leadership, and community development. He served as a band director at Sycamore High School in Cheatham County, Tennessee, building programs from the ground up and leading award-winning ensembles. He was recognized as a national quarterfinalist for the Grammy Music Educator Award — one of the highest honors in music education — by the Recording Academy and GRAMMY Museum in 2024.

A graduate of Elon University and Austin Peay State University, Jordan Tupper was recognized at Elon's Order of Omega Omicron Delta Kappa Leadership Awards in 2004. He has served in civic leadership roles in Pleasant View, Tennessee, and brings the same commitment to community to his work in Baton Rouge.

Throughout his career, Jordan Tupper has developed and led programs across education, nonprofit, and private sectors, with a consistent focus on community impact, stakeholder engagement, and organizational growth.
